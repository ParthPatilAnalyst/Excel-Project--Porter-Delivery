# Porter Delivery: Logistics and Operational Efficiency Analysis

## Project Overview

This project is a comprehensive logistics and operations analysis conducted for **Porter**, a large last-mile delivery and logistics service provider. The study uses operational data to evaluate delivery performance, identify inefficiencies, and recommend improvements in partner allocation and order handling.

The analysis is based on **more than 176,000 delivery records**, covering multiple markets, order protocols, time slots, and operational conditions. The primary aim is to improve delivery speed, reduce delays, and support better workforce planning through data-driven insights.

This project is especially relevant for **operations managers, logistics planners, and business analysts** working in high-volume delivery environments.

---

## Business Problem Statement

In logistics-driven businesses, even small delays can lead to:

* Lower customer satisfaction
* Increased customer complaints and cancellations
* Higher operational costs
* Poor utilization of delivery partners

Porter was facing noticeable fluctuations in delivery performance across different markets and time periods. Delivery lead times were inconsistent, especially during peak hours and weekends. Without a structured analysis, it was difficult to understand:

* Which delivery protocols were inefficient
* Why certain time slots experienced frequent delays
* Whether partner availability matched order demand
* How order size influenced delivery time

This project addresses these issues through systematic data analysis.

---

## STAR Method Breakdown

### Situation

Delivery operations showed uneven performance across cities, market IDs, and time slots. Certain hours experienced high numbers of outstanding orders, while others showed underutilization of delivery partners. Weekend demand, particularly on Sundays, created additional pressure on the system.

These challenges affected service quality and made it difficult for operations teams to plan staffing and delivery strategies effectively.

---

### Task

The key objectives of this project were to:

* Analyze delivery lead time across different markets and order protocols
* Identify time-based trends causing delivery delays
* Measure the impact of order size on actual delivery time
* Evaluate whether delivery partners were optimally allocated during peak hours
* Provide clear, data-backed recommendations to improve operational efficiency

The overall goal was to transform raw delivery data into insights that could directly support operational decision-making.

---

### Action

A structured and step-by-step analytical approach was applied.

#### Lead Time and Protocol Analysis

* Calculated average **Lead Time** across multiple **Market IDs**
* Compared delivery performance across different **Order Protocols**
* Identified protocol-level differences to understand which workflows were faster and more reliable
* Highlighted protocols that consistently resulted in longer delivery times

#### Trend and Time-Based Analysis

* Analyzed delivery time patterns across:

  * Weeks
  * Days of the week
  * Hourly time slots
* Identified **Sunday** as a critical day with frequent delivery delays
* Observed that late-night and early-morning hours had a mismatch between demand and available partners

#### Regression and Impact Analysis

* Performed regression analysis to study the relationship between:

  * Total Items in an order
  * Actual Delivery Time
* Established a baseline relationship to support better delivery time estimation
* Helped quantify how increases in order size impacted delivery duration

#### Peak Hour and Resource Optimization

* Analyzed **Total Busy Partners** against **Outstanding Orders**
* Identified peak hours where demand exceeded supply
* Detected periods of partner underutilization
* Focused on operational gaps during high-demand time slots, especially around **01:00 hours**

---

### Result

The analysis produced clear and actionable outcomes that could be directly implemented:

* Identified **Order Protocol 5** as the most efficient delivery workflow:

  * Average delivery time: **45 minutes**

* Compared with **Order Protocol 1**, which showed:

  * Average delivery time: **50 minutes**

* Highlighted a major operational bottleneck during the **01:00 peak hour**

* Recommended increasing **on-shift delivery partners by 15%** during this time slot

* Expected operational improvements include:

  * Reduction in outstanding orders
  * Faster delivery turnaround times
  * Improved partner utilization
  * Higher customer satisfaction

These insights allowed operations teams to move from reactive problem-solving to proactive planning.
